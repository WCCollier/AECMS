SEO Setup

Help search engines and AI surfaces find your books, articles, and services.

One-time setup  ·  ~30 minutes

✅ What AECMS does for you automatically

Every published page on your site already has accurate <title>, <meta name="description">, and Open Graph tags based on your content. A /sitemap.xml is generated automatically and updates whenever you publish. A /robots.txt is served at the correct URL. You do not need a separate SEO plugin.

This chapter covers the one-time configuration steps that make your author identity legible to Google's entity graph and AI answer engines, and the optional per-content fields you can fill in for fine-grained control.

Step 1 — Fill in your SEO Settings

Go to Admin → Settings → SEO. Fill in every field in this tab — it takes about five minutes and has a meaningful impact on how your site appears in search results and AI-generated answers.

FieldWhat to put
Site NameYour site's public name — e.g. Fantasy V Reality
Site DescriptionOne or two sentences about the site. Used as the default meta description on pages that don't have their own. Keep it under 160 characters.
Default OG Image URLA 1200×630px image for social sharing cards when a page has no cover image. Use a branded image — your book cover, author photo, or site logo. Upload it to your media library first and copy the URL.
Canonical DomainYour primary domain, e.g. https://fantasyvreality.com. This is what appears in canonical link tags — important if you have multiple domains.
Author NameYour author name exactly as it appears on Amazon and Goodreads — e.g. W. C. Collier
Author Page URLThe URL of your author page on this site, or your author alias domain — e.g. https://wccollier.com
Twitter / X HandleYour handle with @, e.g. @wccollier. Used in Twitter Card tags, which also appear in Discord, Slack, and iMessage link previews.
Author Profile URLsOne URL per line — see Step 2 below. This is the most important field for entity recognition.
Google Verification CodeSee Step 3 below. Leave blank until you have it.

Step 2 — Build your Author Profile URL list

The Author Profile URLs field is the key to Google's knowledge graph. When Google sees the same author name linked across multiple authoritative sources — your site, Amazon Author Central, Goodreads, and social profiles — it connects them into a single entity. This is how you get cited in AI-generated answers like "what authors write about X."

Paste one URL per line for every profile you have:

https://www.amazon.com/stores/author/YOUR_AUTHOR_ID
https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/YOUR_GOODREADS_ID
https://www.instagram.com/yourhandle
https://www.tiktok.com/@yourhandle
https://x.com/yourhandle
ℹ️ How to find your Amazon Author Central URL

Go to author.amazon.com and sign in. Your Author Central page URL will be in the format https://www.amazon.com/stores/author/XXXXXXXXXX. Claim your page if you haven't already — it also lets you add a bio, photo, and blog feed that appear on your Amazon book listings.

ℹ️ How to find your Goodreads author URL

Search for your name on goodreads.com. Your author page URL will be in the format https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/XXXXXXX.Your_Name. If you haven't claimed it yet, click "Is this you? Let us know" on your author page listing.

Step 3 — Submit to Google Search Console

ℹ️ Already on Google Cloud Run with a custom domain?

Cloud Run domain mapping requires Google Search Console domain verification as part of setup. If you've already mapped a custom domain to your Cloud Run service, Google has likely already added you as a verified Owner of that property in Search Console — no additional verification needed.

Check first: open Google Search Console and look for your domain under "Search properties." If it's already listed and shows you as Owner, skip straight to Step 6 (submitting the sitemap) — and leave the Google Verification Code field in Admin → Settings → SEO blank.

The steps below apply if Search Console shows your domain as unverified, or if you are adding a second domain that wasn't part of the Cloud Run setup.

  1. Go to Google Search Console and sign in with your Google account.
  2. Click Add Property → URL prefix → enter your site's URL (e.g. https://example.com).
  3. Choose the HTML tag verification method. Google gives you a <meta> tag like:
    <meta name="google-site-verification" content="aBcDeFgHiJkL…" />
  4. Copy just the content value (the part after content=, without quotes) and paste it into Admin → Settings → SEO → Google Verification Code. Save.
  5. Back in Search Console, click Verify. It should confirm immediately.
  6. Go to Sitemaps in Search Console and submit https://yoursite.com/sitemap.xml. Google will crawl it and start indexing your content within a few days.
✅ Also submit to Bing

Go to Bing Webmaster Tools and import your Google Search Console property with one click. Bing powers several other search surfaces (DuckDuckGo, Yahoo, Ecosia) and AI answer engines. The same sitemap URL works.

Step 4 — Add book details to your product listings

When you add a book to the Shop, fill in the Book Details panel on the product edit page. These fields populate a schema.org/Book structured data block that search engines use to identify your product as a book — unlocking rich results and AI citation.

FieldNotes
ISBNUse the ISBN-13 if you have one (starts with 978 or 979). Available from your publisher or your Amazon listing.
FormateBook, Paperback, Hardcover, or Audiobook. Set this to get the bookFormat field in structured data.
Page CountApproximate is fine.
Publishere.g. BookLocker.com, Inc. or Self-published
Amazon URLPaste the full URL of the book's Amazon listing. This is the sameAs link that connects your product page to the Amazon entity — the most impactful single field for entity recognition.
Goodreads URLThe book's Goodreads page URL. Add this once the book appears on Goodreads.
✅ The Amazon URL field is the most important

When your AECMS product page links to the Amazon listing via sameAs, Google's entity graph connects them: "this product and this Amazon book are the same thing." This is what causes your site to appear alongside (or instead of) Amazon in search results for your book title.

Step 5 — Test your structured data

After filling in the Book Details for your first product, verify the structured data is valid:

  1. Go to Google Rich Results Test.
  2. Enter the URL of a product page — e.g. https://fantasyvreality.com/shop/outsiders-vol-1.
  3. The test should show a Book result with no errors. Warnings are acceptable.
  4. Repeat for an article page — it should show an Article result.

Optional — Per-content SEO overrides

Every article, product, and page has a SEO panel in its edit form. The defaults (title, excerpt/description, first image) are usually sufficient — only override when you want something specific for search results.

FieldWhen to use it
Meta TitleWhen the article/product title is too long for a search result (keep under 60 characters) or you want a different title in search than on the page.
Meta DescriptionWhen the excerpt or short description isn't compelling enough as a search snippet. Write it as a call to action. Keep under 160 characters.
OG Image URLWhen the first image isn't the right image for social sharing. A book cover works better than a detail photo for social cards.

The Preview box in the SEO panel shows how your title and description will appear in a Google search result — it updates live as you type.

What to expect

After completing these steps:

  • Within 1–3 days: Google crawls your sitemap and starts indexing your pages.
  • Within 1–2 weeks: Your pages start appearing in search results. Book pages with structured data may show rich results.
  • Within 1–3 months: Google's entity graph begins connecting your author identity across your site, Amazon, and Goodreads. Your name becomes a recognized entity in search and AI systems.
ℹ️ AI answer engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT Search, Google AI Mode)

These systems read your structured data to verify facts and establish entity relationships. Having valid Book schema with ISBNs, and a Person schema linking to Amazon Author Central and Goodreads, meaningfully increases the probability that your books appear in AI-generated reading recommendations — even without high traffic. It is one of the few levers that doesn't require an existing audience to work.
