Chapter 9 — Commerce: Tax & Shipping

Configure your business identity, activate sales tax collection, set shipping rates, and understand the customer account experience.

How Commerce Infrastructure Works

The tax and shipping systems are built into every AECMS deployment but are off by default. All the code, database fields, and UI is present on day one — nothing is installed or upgraded when you activate. Two ISM flags control whether customers see any of it:

FlagDefaultEffect when enabled
tax.enabledfalseTax computed, displayed, and collected on all orders
shipping.enabledfalseShipping cost added to physical-product orders at checkout

The address book and checkout address-collection flow are always active — they are useful for logged-in customers regardless of tax or shipping status.

✅ Ship dark, activate when ready

You can fill in Shop Config, set your tax codes, and configure shipping rates at any time without affecting live customers. Flip the flag when you are legally and operationally ready. No redeploy required for activation.

Shop Config — /admin/shop-config

Gated on the shop.configure capability (Owner only). This is where AECMS learns who your business is — the information that appears on receipts, drives tax nexus determination, and anchors your shipping origin. Fill this in before activating either tax or shipping.

Business Identity

FieldUsed for
Legal business nameAppears on order confirmation emails and tax receipts. May differ from your site title (e.g. "Jane Smith LLC" vs "Jane's Books").
EIN / Tax IDStored encrypted. Required for future 1099 obligations if you pay contractors. Leave blank until you have it.
State tax registration numberYour Comptroller permit number or equivalent. Displayed on tax-inclusive receipts. Required before flipping tax.enabled.
Business addressYour registered legal address. Stripe Tax uses this to determine your nexus — the state(s) where you have a tax obligation.

Shipping Origin

By default, your shipping origin is the same as your business address — check "Same as business address" to use it. If you ship from a warehouse or fulfillment center at a different address, uncheck the box and enter the origin address separately. All shipping calculations use this address for domestic/international rate determination.

Tax section

The Tax section within Shop Config shows your tax toggle and rate settings. Business state and registration number are read from Business Identity above. See Activating Tax for the full walkthrough.

Shipping section

The Shipping section shows your shipping toggle and rate tiers. Shipping origin is read from Business Identity above. See Activating Shipping for the full walkthrough.

Activating Tax Collection

⚠️ Do not activate tax before you are registered

Collecting sales tax without a state permit can expose you to penalties. Complete the prerequisite steps below before flipping tax.enabled = true.

Prerequisites (non-code steps)

  1. Determine your nexus. You are typically obligated to collect sales tax in states where you have a physical presence (home office, warehouse). For most single-owner sites, this is one state.
  2. Register with your state tax authority. In Texas, this is the Texas Comptroller. Registration is free and takes a few days. You will receive a permit number — keep it handy.
  3. Enable Stripe Tax in the Stripe Dashboard. Log in → Tax → Enable Stripe Tax → enter your business address. Stripe uses this to compute the correct rate for each jurisdiction automatically, including local rates. This step is required even if all your orders come through Stripe Checkout — Stripe Tax is not on by default.

Configuring tax in Shop Config

  1. Go to Admin → Shop Config → Tax.
  2. Enter your state tax registration number (from registration step above) in Business Identity.
  3. Set a Default Stripe tax code — the Stripe tax code used for any product that does not have one set on its own. Common codes:
    Product typeStripe tax code
    Physical goods (general)txcd_99999999
    Digital books / ebookstxcd_10010001
    Digital audiotxcd_10020000
    Digital videotxcd_10040000
    Online coursestxcd_10070000
    Servicestxcd_20030000
  4. Set the PayPal flat rate — the flat tax percentage applied to PayPal orders (e.g. 8.25 for Texas). PayPal cannot call Stripe Tax automatically, so AECMS applies this flat rate to all PayPal transactions when tax is enabled.

Setting per-product tax codes

Open any product in the Product editor. Under the Details panel, find the Tax Code dropdown. Select the code that matches this product's taxability. Products with no code set use the Default Stripe Tax Code from Shop Config.

Activating

  1. Confirm Business address and state are filled in under Shop Config → Business Identity.
  2. Confirm Stripe Tax is enabled in your Stripe Dashboard (Stripe Dashboard → Tax → settings show "Enabled").
  3. In Shop Config → Tax, flip Tax collection enabled to on and click Save.

Tax collection goes live immediately on the next customer purchase. Stripe Checkout will compute and display the correct tax based on the customer's location. The tax amount is stored on every order and appears on the customer's confirmation email and order page.

ℹ️ Filing tax returns

AECMS collects tax on your behalf — you are responsible for remitting it to your state tax authority on the schedule they assign (typically quarterly or annually for low-volume sellers). Use the Tax Report in the Backstage to get your totals per period.

Activating Shipping

AECMS supports flat-rate shipping tiers. You define up to two domestic rates (standard and priority) and an optional international rate. Per-product overrides are available for items that ship differently.

Setting shipping rates

  1. Go to Admin → Shop Config → Shipping.
  2. Confirm your shipping origin is correct under Business Identity (or fill it in if different from your business address).
  3. Enter Tier 1 — Standard: a label (e.g. "Standard Shipping") and a rate in cents (e.g. 799 for $7.99).
  4. Optionally enter Tier 2 — Priority for an express option.
  5. Optionally enter a Free shipping threshold in cents — orders above this subtotal get free shipping (e.g. 5000 for "free over $50").
  6. Optionally enter an International rate if you ship outside your home country.
✅ How to price shipping

Look up USPS Priority Mail flat-rate box costs from your zip code to a few representative destinations. Round up a dollar or two. For most low-volume sellers, this is simpler and more predictable than real-time carrier rates.

Per-product overrides

Open a Physical product in the Product editor. Find the Shipping Cost Override field (only shown for physical products). Enter a cost in cents. When set, this exact amount is charged for shipping this product — ignoring the global tiers. Useful for oversized or unusually heavy items.

Leave blank on most products to use the global tier rate.

Activating

  1. In Shop Config → Shipping, flip Shipping enabled to on and click Save.

Shipping costs appear immediately on the cart page and at checkout for any order containing physical products. The shipping amount is stored on the order and shown to the customer on the confirmation email and their order detail page.

Tax Report — /admin/tax-report

The Tax Report gives you the numbers you need to file your periodic tax return.

  • Date range picker — defaults to the current calendar quarter. Set any custom range to match your filing period.
  • Total sales (pre-tax) — the sum of all order subtotals in the period.
  • Total tax collected — the sum of all tax_amount values on completed orders.
  • State breakdown — tax collected per jurisdiction (from Stripe Tax's breakdown data).
  • CSV export — download the breakdown for your accountant or tax filing software.
ℹ️ Tax report only includes orders where tax was collected

Orders placed before tax.enabled was flipped, or PayPal orders with a zero flat rate, will show tax_amount = 0 and are excluded from the tax totals. This is correct — no tax was collected on those orders.

Customer Account Page — /account

Logged-in customers manage their own data at /account. As the site owner, you are also a customer — this is where you update your own display name, manage saved addresses, and control notification preferences.

Edit Profile

The Edit Profile section (collapsible, below the read-only Profile display) lets any logged-in user update their first name, last name, and username. Only fields you fill in are changed — leave a field blank to keep the current value. Names are stored encrypted at rest.

✅ Set your display name here

After initial setup your display name may be blank. Go to /account → Edit Profile and enter your first and last name. This name appears in the admin sidebar and on any content you author.

Saved Addresses

The Saved Addresses section lets customers save shipping addresses for faster checkout. Addresses are stored encrypted at rest. Customers can add multiple addresses, set a default, and delete old ones. At checkout, the default address is offered with a "Use this / Change" option.

Notifications

Subscription preferences for optional marketing emails — new articles, new products, news & alerts. System emails (order confirmation, password reset, email verification) cannot be disabled. Customers control their preferences here; you control the defaults in Settings → General → Subscription Defaults.

Order History

Shows the customer's 5 most recent orders with status and a link to the order detail page. Customers can also view all orders from the order detail link.

Digital Library

Appears automatically for customers who have purchased digital products. Shows all their owned files with download links. Download tokens are time-limited and regenerated on each access.

Change Password & Delete Account

Standard self-service account management. Changing the password revokes all active sessions and redirects to login. Account deletion is a soft-delete — the record is retained for order history integrity.
